说一句话，得到一个机器验收过的软件版本Say one sentence, get a machine-verified build back

全流程公开 · 成品在线可试Fully public · results playable online

想试一个产品新点子，通常得工程师改代码、反复调、再看效果，每试一次都又慢又贵。这条流水线把它压成一句话的事：人用一句话说想要什么，AI 生成完整的新版本，机器自动试跑 200 局做验收——合格的放行，不合格的自动打回重做，还不行就淘汰，全程不用人盯。有一个做砸了的版本就是这么被自动拦下的，没混进成品。我用一个小游戏当试验对象，所以每个验收合格的版本，在网页上就能亲手试到。

Trying a new product idea normally means an engineer coding it, tuning it, checking the result — slow and expensive per attempt. This pipeline compresses that into one sentence: you say what you want in plain words, an AI builds a complete new version, and a machine test-runs it 200 rounds to accept or reject — passes ship, failures get reworked or dropped, no human supervision. One botched version was caught and discarded exactly this way. I used a small game as the test subject, so every version that passed is playable in your browser.

让 AI 进真实系统干活，又闯不了祸AI working inside a real system — without the risk

代码公开 · 可现场演示 / 录屏Code public · live demo / screencast on request

企业最想让 AI 接手的是值班、故障处理这类活，也最怕它乱来。这个智能体接到故障告警后，自动判断是什么问题、该派给谁；高风险的操作会强制停下来等人签字，每一步都留记录，判断准确率有自动化测试卡着——低于 95% 系统直接亮红灯、不许上线。这套"放 AI 干活、但关得住它"的做法，来自我此前给企业建安全运营中心（SOC）的经验。

Duty rotations and incident handling are the jobs companies most want AI to take — and where they most fear it going rogue. This agent takes a fault alert, works out what it is and who should handle it; anything high-risk is stopped for a human signature, every step is logged, and its judgment is held to an automated test — below 95% accuracy, it's blocked from shipping. This "let AI work, but keep it contained" approach comes from my experience building an enterprise security operations center (SOC).

For most people, every AI session means onboarding an amnesiac new hire all over again. I built a system that fixes this for myself: experience, methods and past decisions from all my projects, stored in a structure the AI can draw on directly — it understands me better the longer we work, and derives new conclusions from what it already knows. I run on it daily; it's not a concept deck. Its generic parts are published as four open-source tools anyone can use.
